### **AI Fundamentals**

- **Definition:** AI (Artificial Intelligence) refers to systems designed to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as reasoning, problem-solving, pattern recognition, and language understanding.
- **Key Types:**
    - **Machine Learning (ML):** Learns patterns from data to make predictions or decisions.
    - **Neural Networks:** Layered structures mimicking brain ‚Äúneurons‚Äù that transform inputs into outputs.
    - **Language Models (LLMs):** Predict and generate text, summarize, or answer questions by recognizing language patterns.

### **Everyday AI Examples**

- Music/movie recommendations: Spotify, Netflix
- Navigation & traffic: Google Maps, Waze
- Smart assistants: Alexa, Google Home, Siri
- Email spam detection & smart replies
- Photo editing & enhancement apps

### **Prompting Tips**

- **Be Specific:** Clear instructions produce better results.
- **Provide Context:** Explain background, constraints, or audience.
- **State Your Goal:** Clarify what outcome you want.
- **Specify Format:** Bullet points, stories, tables, code snippets, etc.

**Example:**

- Weak: ‚ÄúWrite about space.‚Äù
- Strong: ‚ÄúWrite a 150-word inspirational summary about why space exploration matters, aimed at high-school students, in bullet points.‚Äù

### **AI Strengths**

- Speed and efficiency in processing information
- Summarizing large amounts of data
- Pattern recognition and analysis
- Brainstorming ideas quickly
- Rewriting, structuring, or formatting content

### **AI Limitations**

- Cannot feel emotions or understand human experiences
- Cannot make moral or ethical judgments independently
- Lacks personal lived experience
- May not have up-to-date information

### **Ethics & Responsible Use**

- Detect and mitigate bias (gender, race, age, socioeconomic factors)
- Understand what data is collected and how it is used
- Humans provide judgment, empathy, and context
- Treat AI as a **collaborative tool**, not a replacement for human decision-making

### **AI Mindset**

- **Curious:** Explore AI‚Äôs capabilities, ask ‚Äúwhat if?‚Äù
- **Skeptical:** Question outputs, check for errors and bias
- **Creative:** Use AI to inspire original thinking
- **Responsible:** Consider ethical and privacy implications
- **Collaborative:** Combine AI efficiency with human insight
