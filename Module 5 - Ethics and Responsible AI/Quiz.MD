# üöÄ **QUIZ 3 ‚Äî Challenge & Reflection (7 Questions)**

### **1. Short Answer:**

Give one example of how biased data could lead to unfair AI decisions.

**Expected Response:**
A resume-screening AI may favor candidates similar to previous hires, disadvantaging women or minorities.

---

### **2. Scenario:**

An AI app collects your location and voice data. What steps should you take to use it responsibly?

**Expected Response:**
Check privacy settings, understand data storage, consent to data sharing, and avoid sharing sensitive information if uncomfortable.

---

### **3. Short Answer:**

Name two strengths of AI and two limitations that require human oversight.

**Expected Response:**
Strengths: speed, pattern recognition.
Limitations: ethical reasoning, empathy, subjective judgment.

---

### **4. Reflection:**

Discuss a real-world situation where human + AI collaboration improves outcomes.

**Expected Response:**
Examples: doctors reviewing AI-predicted diagnoses, financial analysts checking fraud detection alerts, or teachers personalizing learning with AI recommendations.

---

### **5. Scenario:**

Should AI ever make life-changing decisions without human input? Explain your reasoning.

**Expected Response:**
Generally no; humans are needed to provide ethical judgment, context, and oversight to prevent harm.

---

### **6. Critical Thinking:**

How can organizations reduce bias in AI systems?

**Expected Response:**
Use diverse and representative data, regularly audit AI outputs, implement fairness metrics, and involve humans in reviewing AI decisions.

---

### **7. Challenge:**

Compare two AI systems: one trained on biased historical data and one on diverse, inclusive data. How might their outputs differ?

**Expected Response:**
The biased system may unfairly disadvantage certain groups, while the inclusive system is more equitable and accurate across demographics.
