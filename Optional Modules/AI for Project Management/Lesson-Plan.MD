# **Lesson Plan: AI for Project Management**

**Target Audience:** Project managers, PMO staff, team leads, and project coordinators
**Duration:** 2–3 hours (can be adjusted for half-day or full-day sessions)
**Delivery Mode:** Instructor-led, online, or hybrid

---

## **Learning Objectives**

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

1. Explain how AI supports project management workflows.
2. Identify areas where AI can automate, augment, or accelerate tasks.
3. Evaluate AI tools and platforms for project planning, resource management, risk mitigation, and reporting.
4. Apply AI insights to improve decision-making, forecasting, and team collaboration.
5. Recognize when human oversight is critical and AI should not replace decision-making.

## **Learning Activities**

1. **Interactive Discussion:** Ask participants which project management tasks are repetitive or data-heavy in their experience.
2. **Case Studies:** Show real-life AI use cases in scheduling, resource allocation, and risk prediction.
3. **Hands-on Exercises:** Demonstrate AI tools (like Asana AI or Power BI AI) with sample project data.
4. **Role-Play:** Simulate a project meeting where AI generates action items and summaries.
5. **Group Workshop:** Complete the AI suitability table for participants’ own PM tasks.

---

## **Assessment & Evaluation**

* **Formative:** Group discussions, hands-on exercises, Q&A during sessions
* **Summative:** Short multiple-choice or scenario-based quiz at the end of the session
* **Practical Evaluation:** Completion of AI suitability table and suggested workflow improvements

---

## **Materials & Resources**

* Slides / visual deck of AI in PM
* Whiteboard or online collaboration tools (Miro, MURAL)
* Access to AI-enabled PM platforms (Microsoft Project AI, Asana AI, Smartsheet AI)
* Cheat sheet: “AI in Project Management” one-page reference
* Sample project datasets for demonstrations

---

## **Follow-Up & Reinforcement**

* Encourage participants to test AI features in their current projects.
* Distribute the one-page AI cheat sheet.
* Schedule a follow-up session to share lessons learned, success stories, and AI adoption tips.
