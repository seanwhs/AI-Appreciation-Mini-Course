# **AI for Trainers & Facilitators — Quiz Set**

## **Section 1: Basic Concepts**

**Q1 — Multiple Choice:**
Which of the following tasks is **most suitable for AI assistance** in training?
A. Evaluating learners’ emotional engagement in real-time
B. Creating a draft lesson plan outline
C. Making final strategic decisions on training strategy
D. Conducting high-level negotiation with stakeholders

**Answer:** B

---

**Q2 — True/False:**
AI can fully replace a trainer in delivering interactive workshops to diverse audiences.
**Answer:** False

---

**Q3 — Multiple Choice:**
Why is domain expertise essential for trainers using AI?
A. To reduce AI computing costs
B. To validate AI-generated content for accuracy and relevance
C. To prevent AI from generating any content
D. To automate all assessment grading

**Answer:** B

---

## **Section 2: Content Creation & Customization**

**Q4 — Scenario-Based:**
You use AI to generate a 45-minute lesson on cybersecurity basics. The AI content contains outdated statistics and some minor technical inaccuracies. What should you do?
A. Deliver the lesson as-is to save time
B. Review, correct inaccuracies, and update statistics before delivering
C. Delete all AI content and create a lesson manually
D. Only use AI content for slides, ignoring text

**Answer:** B

---

**Q5 — Multiple Choice:**
Which AI-generated elements still **require trainer customization**?
A. Case studies and scenarios
B. Quiz questions
C. Slide bullet points
D. All of the above

**Answer:** D

---

**Q6 — True/False:**
AI can suggest interactive activities for learners, but trainers must evaluate if they fit the specific audience.
**Answer:** True

---

## **Section 3: Assessment & Validation**

**Q7 — Multiple Choice:**
When designing assessments using AI, which step is **critical**?
A. Checking for alignment with learning objectives
B. Ensuring questions are free of bias and culturally appropriate
C. Validating answer keys and rationales
D. All of the above

**Answer:** D

---

**Q8 — Scenario-Based:**
AI generates multiple-choice questions on leadership skills. You notice some questions are too easy and don’t challenge learners. What is the best approach?
A. Use the questions as-is
B. Edit the questions to increase difficulty and align with objectives
C. Remove all AI-generated questions and start from scratch
D. Only use the AI questions for homework

**Answer:** B

---

## **Section 4: Ethical & Practical Use**

**Q9 — True/False:**
Trainers should verify AI-generated content to avoid spreading misinformation or outdated practices.
**Answer:** True

---

**Q10 — Multiple Choice:**
Which of the following is **not** a recommended best practice for trainers using AI?
A. Using AI as a co-pilot for drafting content
B. Blindly trusting AI outputs without review
C. Customizing AI suggestions for audience needs
D. Keeping a library of effective AI prompts

**Answer:** B

---

## **Section 5: Reflection & Strategy**

**Q11 — Short Answer / Reflection:**
List two ways AI can save time for trainers **and** two areas where human expertise is still essential.

**Sample Answer:**
*Time-saving:* Drafting lesson plans, generating quizzes.
*Human expertise:* Validating content accuracy, adapting activities to learner needs.

---

**Q12 — Scenario-Based:**
You want to create a role-play scenario for conflict resolution training. AI generates a scenario with generic roles and problems. How should you proceed?

* Evaluate and customize roles and context to match your learners
* Adjust the challenge level to align with skill objectives
* Ensure cultural and organizational relevance

**Answer:** All of the above
