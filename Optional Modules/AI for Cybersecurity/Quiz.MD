## **Section 3 ‚Äî Short Answer (5 Questions)**

1. Name two AI platforms used for agentic Blue Team simulations.
   **Sample Answer:** MITRE Caldera, AttackIQ

2. How does AI help reduce false positives in SOC environments?
   **Sample Answer:** By correlating logs, enriching alerts with context, identifying patterns, and deduplicating similar events

3. What is one key difference between standard AI detection tools and agentic AI in cybersecurity?
   **Sample Answer:** Agentic AI autonomously generates attack scenarios and TTP simulations, while standard AI primarily analyzes existing alerts

4. Give one example of a hands-on exercise using AI for cybersecurity learning.
   **Sample Answer:** AI-simulated phishing campaign for detection and containment practice

5. Why is human oversight essential even when using agentic AI for Blue Team exercises?
   **Sample Answer:** Humans interpret context, make strategic decisions, validate mitigations, and ensure safe application of AI insights

## **Section 4 ‚Äî Application / Scenario (3 Questions)**

1. A SOC receives AI-enriched alerts about unusual network activity. Describe how analysts should proceed.
   **Sample Answer:** Review AI context and enrichment, validate anomalies, correlate with other logs, escalate if necessary, and update detection rules

2. Your Blue Team wants to test defenses against new ransomware tactics. How can agentic AI assist?
   **Sample Answer:** Simulate ransomware behavior in a safe test network, provide logs for analysis, suggest mitigations, and map TTPs to MITRE ATT&CK

3. During a week-long AI-driven Red/Blue simulation, what steps should analysts take to improve defenses?
   **Sample Answer:** Daily review of AI attack logs, tune detection rules, test alerts, refine playbooks, and conduct Purple Team reviews
