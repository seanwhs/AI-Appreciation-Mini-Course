### **Lesson Plan for AI in Cybersecurity**

---

### **Module Title:** AI for Cybersecurity

### **Duration:** 2-3 Hours

### **Goal of This Module:**

Equip learners to understand:

* How AI strengthens modern cybersecurity programs.
* Where AI is deployed across defensive workflows.
* Which tools and platforms incorporate AI capabilities.
* What tasks AI can automate, augment, or accelerate.
* How cybersecurity analysts can partner with AI to make networks safer, responses faster, and risks smaller.
* How agentic AI supports Blue Team exercises to proactively detect, simulate, and mitigate evolving attack TTPs.
* How hands-on simulations accelerate learning through AI-driven Red/Blue team exercises.

---

### **Learning Objectives:**

By the end of this module, learners will:

1. Understand the role of AI in modern cybersecurity.
2. Be able to identify tasks in cybersecurity workflows that can be augmented by AI.
3. Learn how agentic AI can enhance Blue Team training and defense tactics.
4. Participate in hands-on simulations using AI tools to simulate Red/Blue team exercises.
5. Gain insights into integrating AI into Security Operations Center (SOC) workflows for proactive threat detection and incident response.

---

### **Materials Needed:**

* Computers/tablets with access to AI-driven cybersecurity platforms (SIEM/EDR, etc.).
* Access to simulation tools (e.g., MITRE Caldera, AttackIQ, Cymulate).
* Sample datasets (e.g., logs, email data, process trees for ransomware).
* Projector or screen for presentations and demos.
* Whiteboard/markers for brainstorming and mapping workflows.
* Handouts: AI for Cybersecurity Cheat Sheet, SOC AI Training Map.

---

### **Module Breakdown:**

---

#### **1. Introduction to AI in Cybersecurity (30 minutes)**

**Objective:** Introduce learners to the landscape of AI in cybersecurity and its importance.

1. **AI in Cybersecurity — The Big Picture:**

   * Cybersecurity is one of the most AI-dependent fields, where AI helps in managing massive data, real-time monitoring, and evolving threats.
   * AI automates repetitive, time-sensitive tasks and helps reduce analyst burnout while strengthening defenses.
   * **AI vs. Humans:** AI handles high-volume data and pattern recognition, while humans manage judgment, strategy, and adversarial analysis.

2. **AI Capabilities in Cybersecurity:**

   * **Automates:** Continuous data analysis, threat detection, and event correlation.
   * **Augments:** Security analysts' abilities by reducing noise, detecting anomalies, and predicting threats.
   * **Agentic AI**: Uses autonomous simulations to train Blue Teams and improve defense strategies.

3. **Real-world Applications:**

   * Nearly all modern cybersecurity tools (SIEM, EDR, firewalls) use AI for real-time threat detection and response.
   * Examples: Detecting anomalies in network traffic, phishing attacks, and identifying malware.

---

#### **2. AI Tools & Platforms in Cybersecurity (45 minutes)**

**Objective:** Explore real-world AI tools and platforms that are commonly used in cybersecurity.

1. **Threat Detection with AI:**

   * **AI in SIEM/EDR:** Tools like **Splunk**, **CrowdStrike**, and **Darktrace** use machine learning to detect threats based on event patterns.
   * **Hands-on Demo:** Learners use an AI-powered SIEM dashboard to detect suspicious activity, such as abnormal login patterns or network anomalies.

2. **Phishing Detection:**

   * **AI in Email Security:** Platforms like **Proofpoint** and **Cofense** use AI to analyze email content, detect phishing attempts, and reduce false positives.
   * **Hands-on Exercise:** Learners review AI-generated phishing emails and identify patterns of suspicious behavior.

3. **Agentic AI for Blue Team Training:**

   * **AI-Generated Attack Simulations:** Platforms like **MITRE Caldera**, **Cymulate**, and **AttackIQ** simulate advanced attack vectors and tactics.
   * **Hands-on Activity:** Learners simulate a Red Team attack on a Blue Team environment, identifying vulnerabilities and responding to AI-generated attack scenarios.

4. **Incident Response & Containment:**

   * **AI in SOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response):** Tools like **Palo Alto Networks Cortex XSOAR** help automate responses to incidents such as ransomware or data exfiltration.
   * **Exercise:** Learners work through a ransomware incident simulation using AI-driven SOAR tools to isolate and contain the attack.

---

#### **3. Case Studies & Hands-On AI-Driven Simulations (45 minutes)**

**Objective:** Provide practical, hands-on experiences with agentic AI-driven simulations to strengthen incident response and Blue Team capabilities.

1. **Case Study 1: Synthetic Phishing Attack Simulation**

   * **Scenario:** An AI agent simulates a spear-phishing attack targeting the finance department.
   * **Objective:** Detect the phishing attempt, trace lateral movement, and mitigate future threats.
   * **Exercise Steps:**

     * Review AI-generated phishing emails and identify suspicious elements.
     * Trace the affected endpoints using EDR logs.
     * Deploy automated containment rules and prevent future attacks.
     * Map attacker TTPs to MITRE ATT&CK.
   * **Learning Outcome:** Learners practice rapid detection, automated response, and TTP mapping using AI-driven outputs.

2. **Case Study 2: AI-Generated Ransomware Simulation**

   * **Scenario:** AI simulates ransomware behavior on a small test network.
   * **Objective:** Contain the ransomware and preserve business continuity.
   * **Exercise Steps:**

     * Observe abnormal file encryption patterns flagged by AI.
     * Analyze the process trees and network behavior to identify the attack's origin.
     * Use SOAR tools to isolate affected endpoints and halt the propagation.
     * Review AI-recommended post-incident hardening steps.
   * **Learning Outcome:** Learners gain hands-on experience in incident response augmented by AI insights.

3. **Case Study 3: Red/Blue Continuous Simulation**

   * **Scenario:** AI runs a persistent Red Team campaign over several days, with a Blue Team tasked to respond.
   * **Objective:** Improve detection rules, alert accuracy, and defense readiness.
   * **Exercise Steps:**

     * Review AI-generated attack logs and refine detection rules in SIEM/EDR platforms.
     * Test defenses against AI-simulated lateral movement and privilege escalation.
     * Conduct a Purple Team review: compare AI Red Team tactics to Blue Team responses.
   * **Learning Outcome:** Learners experience iterative improvements in defensive posture, detection, and response through continuous simulation.

---

#### **4. Activity — “Where Can AI Strengthen Your Security Program?” (30 minutes)**

**Objective:** Help learners identify cybersecurity tasks that can be enhanced or automated with AI.

1. **Task Identification:**

   * Learners list 5–7 cybersecurity tasks they perform regularly.
   * Evaluate each task based on AI suitability criteria:

     * High-volume logs
     * Real-time monitoring requirements
     * Pattern-based analysis
     * Repetitive triage tasks
     * False positive overload
     * High risk if delayed
     * Heavy investigation/documentation burden
   * Tasks meeting **2+ criteria** are strong candidates for AI augmentation, including **agentic AI for Blue Team training** and **hands-on Red/Blue exercises**.

2. **Group Discussion:**

   * Share insights on where AI could be implemented within their own security teams and the benefits that could be realized.

---

#### **5. Wrap-Up & Reflection (15 minutes)**

**Objective:** Summarize key takeaways and provide additional resources for further exploration.

1. **Review Key Concepts:**

   * AI is deeply embedded in modern cybersecurity workflows, enhancing threat detection, incident response, and training.
   * **Agentic AI** significantly strengthens Blue Teams by simulating evolving attack techniques and providing hands-on defense practice.
   * Analysts remain essential for strategic decision-making, but AI accelerates their effectiveness and reduces the workload.

2. **Q&A Session:**

   * Open the floor for any final questions or clarifications on AI in cybersecurity.

3. **Closing Remarks:**

   * Encourage learners to explore AI tools available for cybersecurity and experiment with integrating AI into their security programs.

This lesson plan provides a comprehensive approach to integrating AI in cybersecurity practices, from understanding its core functionalities to applying it through real-world simulations. The module emphasizes hands-on experience, team-based learning, and continuous improvement through agentic AI.
