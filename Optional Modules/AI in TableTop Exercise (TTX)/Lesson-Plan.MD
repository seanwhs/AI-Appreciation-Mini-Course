# **Lesson Plan: AI in Tabletop Exercises (TTX)**

## **Course Title:**

AI in Tabletop Exercises: Enhancing Cybersecurity Incident Response and Training

## **Objective:**

Equip participants with the knowledge and skills to leverage AI in Tabletop Exercises (TTX) for improved incident response, training effectiveness, and decision-making.

---

### **Course Duration:**

2 hours (can be extended based on depth of discussion or interactive activities)

---

### **Target Audience:**

Cybersecurity professionals, incident response teams, security operations center (SOC) members, and IT staff interested in learning how AI can be integrated into tabletop exercises to improve the simulation and response process.

---

### **Prerequisites:**

Basic understanding of cybersecurity concepts, tabletop exercises, and incident response procedures.

---

### **Learning Outcomes:**

By the end of the lesson, learners will be able to:

1. **Identify the role of AI in Tabletop Exercises (TTX)**, including the tasks AI can automate, augment, or optimize.
2. **Understand the benefits of AI-driven TTX**, from scenario generation to post-exercise analysis.
3. **Evaluate and implement AI tools** that can enhance TTXs, including real-time threat intelligence integration, adaptive attack simulations, and performance tracking.
4. **Recognize AI's value in improving team collaboration** during a TTX, ensuring more realistic and dynamic simulations.

---

### **Lesson Breakdown:**

#### **1. Introduction to Tabletop Exercises (TTX)** (10 minutes)

* **Objective:** Provide a quick overview of TTX and its importance in cybersecurity incident response.
* **Discussion:**

  * What are TTX?
  * Why are TTXs crucial for cybersecurity preparedness?
  * Traditional vs. AI-enhanced TTXs.
* **Key Points:**

  * TTXs help simulate cybersecurity incidents in a safe environment.
  * They assess team readiness, decision-making, and coordination across departments.
  * AI can enhance TTX by making simulations more dynamic, adaptive, and realistic.

---

#### **2. How AI Can Enhance TTX** (20 minutes)

* **Objective:** Explain how AI can improve various stages of a TTX, from scenario generation to reporting.
* **Presentation:**

  * **Scenario Generation & Simulation**: How AI generates dynamic, evolving attack scenarios based on real-world threat intelligence.
  * **Real-Time Injects & Adaptive Attack Simulations**: AI simulates adaptive adversary behavior, continuously changing the scenario based on team responses.
  * **Incident Documentation & Reporting**: AI automates incident documentation, providing real-time feedback and post-exercise reports.
  * **Threat Intelligence Integration**: AI enriches simulations with real-time threat data from external feeds.
  * **Facilitator Assistance & Team Collaboration**: AI provides facilitators with insights into team performance and suggests improvements.
* **Interactive Example:** Show how AI-based scenario simulations can evolve in response to team actions in real time.

---

#### **3. AI Tools for TTX** (20 minutes)

* **Objective:** Introduce specific AI tools and platforms that are useful for TTX.
* **Presentation:**

  * **SimSpace**, **CyberBit**, **Cortex XSOAR**, **Splunk SOAR**: These tools automate scenario generation, real-time incident management, and after-action reports.
  * **Recorded Future**, **ThreatConnect**, **Anomali**: Tools for integrating threat intelligence into TTX to create more relevant and adaptive scenarios.
  * **Power BI**, **Tableau**: Platforms for post-exercise reporting and performance analytics.
  * **Darktrace**, **Exabeam**: AI for monitoring team responses and suggesting real-time adjustments to the scenario.
* **Real-World Examples:**

  * Show how **Cortex XSOAR** orchestrates an incident from detection to response in a TTX environment.
  * Demonstrate **Recorded Future**’s threat intelligence dashboard in action, feeding real-time data into the exercise.

---

#### **4. Hands-On Activity: AI in Action** (30 minutes)

* **Objective:** Allow participants to experience how AI enhances a TTX through an interactive activity.
* **Activity Setup:**

  * **Scenario:** A simulated phishing attack leads to a data breach and ransomware deployment.
  * **Task:** Participants will use an AI-powered platform (or mock platform) to:

    * Review a dynamically evolving attack scenario.
    * Identify real-time threat intelligence injects.
    * Document decisions and responses via AI-powered tools.
    * Work in teams to strategize responses to the simulated attack.
* **Role of AI:**

  * AI will suggest attack vector changes based on team actions (e.g., lateral movement detected leads to AI introducing new attack types).
  * AI will analyze the team’s performance in real time, generating insights about gaps in communication or decision-making.
  * AI tools will provide automated reports on team actions, highlighting successes and areas for improvement.

---

#### **5. Debrief and Discussion** (15 minutes)

* **Objective:** Reflect on the activity and discuss the benefits and challenges of using AI in TTX.
* **Discussion Points:**

  * How did AI change the experience of the TTX?
  * What insights were gained through the use of AI tools?
  * How did AI assist in decision-making, documentation, and performance evaluation?
  * In what areas did AI add value, and where did human judgment remain critical?
* **Key Takeaways:**

  * AI can significantly improve TTX by making the scenarios more realistic and adaptive.
  * AI tools streamline incident documentation, threat intelligence integration, and performance tracking, saving time and increasing efficiency.

---

#### **6. Conclusion and Q&A** (15 minutes)

* **Objective:** Wrap up the lesson by summarizing key takeaways and addressing any remaining questions.
* **Summary:**

  * AI enhances TTX by automating routine tasks and providing real-time feedback to facilitators and participants.
  * Integrating AI into TTX improves realism, collaboration, and learning outcomes.
  * AI tools can be leveraged for scenario creation, real-time injects, incident documentation, and post-exercise performance analysis.
* **Q&A Session:**

  * Open the floor to questions. Address any concerns or topics of interest from participants.

---

### **Materials Needed:**

* **Presentation Slides** (covering the key topics)
* **AI-powered TTX Platform Demo** or access to mock platforms
* **Scenario Templates** for hands-on activity
* **Computers/Tablets** for participants to interact with the AI tools during the activity
* **Post-exercise Report Templates** (auto-generated by AI tools)

---

### **Assessment:**

* **Activity Completion**: Participants’ ability to interact with AI tools and simulate an incident response during the hands-on activity.
* **Debrief Discussion**: Participation in the debrief and ability to apply AI concepts to real-world TTX scenarios.
* **Post-Course Quiz (Optional)**: A short quiz to assess knowledge retention about AI’s role in enhancing TTX and familiarity with AI tools.

---

### **Optional Homework/Extended Learning:**

* **Explore AI Platforms**: Encourage learners to explore and experiment with the AI tools discussed in the lesson, such as **Cortex XSOAR** or **Recorded Future**. Participants can sign up for trial versions or demo accounts to get hands-on experience.
* **Readings/Resources**: Provide additional readings or resources on AI in cybersecurity and TTX, such as industry whitepapers or case studies.

---

### **Final Remarks:**

By leveraging AI in Tabletop Exercises, organizations can significantly enhance their cybersecurity readiness, training realism, and response efficiency. The key takeaway is that while AI automates many aspects of TTX, human expertise in strategy, decision-making, and coordination remains essential. The combination of both will elevate the incident response capabilities of any organization.
