### **Lesson Plan for AI in Healthcare**

---

### **Module Title:** AI for Healthcare

### **Duration:** 2.5–3 hours

### **Goal of This Module:**

Equip learners to recognize:

* **Where AI already appears in healthcare workflows**
* **Which tools and platforms enable AI-assisted clinical, administrative, and operational tasks**
* **What tasks AI can automate, augment, or accelerate**
* **How healthcare professionals can partner with AI** to improve patient care, reduce errors, and enhance operational efficiency
* **How AI can assist in compliance, population health, and predictive modeling**

---

### **Learning Objectives:**

By the end of this module, learners will:

1. Understand the role of AI in healthcare workflows.
2. Recognize tools and platforms that embed AI to enhance clinical, administrative, and operational efficiency.
3. Identify AI-driven solutions for improving clinical documentation, patient scheduling, diagnostic assistance, and patient care.
4. Gain hands-on experience using AI tools for predictive modeling, workflow optimization, and risk stratification.
5. Learn how AI can support healthcare professionals in clinical decision-making, fraud detection, and patient outcomes prediction.

---

### **Materials Needed:**

* Computers or tablets with access to AI tools (such as Nuance Dragon Medical One, Olive AI, or Aidoc).
* Sample datasets (e.g., clinical documentation, patient risk scores, scheduling data).
* Whiteboard or projector for interactive discussions and presentations.
* Handouts: **AI in Healthcare Cheat Sheet**, **Sample Datasets**, **Case Study Materials**.
* Group activity worksheets for role-playing scenarios.

---

### **Module Breakdown:**

---

#### **1. Introduction to AI in Healthcare (30 minutes)**

**Objective:** Provide an overview of how AI is transforming healthcare and its impact on clinical and operational workflows.

1. **AI in Healthcare — The Big Picture:**

   * Healthcare is increasingly data-driven and relies on large datasets (EHRs, claims data, medical images) and repetitive tasks (documentation, coding).
   * **AI automates:** High-volume, repetitive, error-prone tasks (coding, scheduling, triage).
   * **Humans handle:** Clinical judgment, patient communication, complex diagnostics, and ethical decisions.
   * AI **enhances** clinical decision-making, operational efficiency, and patient care without replacing healthcare professionals.

2. **Key Areas Where AI Shows Up in Healthcare:**

   * Clinical documentation and coding
   * Diagnostic and imaging assistance
   * Patient scheduling and workflow optimization
   * Population health and risk stratification
   * Remote patient monitoring and telehealth
   * Fraud detection and compliance monitoring
   * Claims and revenue cycle optimization

3. **Real-World Examples:**

   * **Clinical documentation:** Nuance Dragon Medical and Olive AI for auto-populating clinical notes.
   * **Imaging assistance:** Aidoc, Zebra Medical Vision, and PathAI for automated diagnostic insights.
   * **Scheduling optimization:** Qventus and LeanTaaS iQueue for predictive scheduling and patient flow management.

---

#### **2. AI Tools & Platforms in Healthcare (45 minutes)**

**Objective:** Dive into AI tools and platforms used in healthcare to improve patient care, operational efficiency, and compliance.

1. **Clinical Documentation & Coding Automation:**

   * **What AI Does:** Transcribes patient encounters, suggests ICD-10 and CPT codes, and ensures compliance.
   * **Example Tools:** Nuance Dragon Medical One, 3M CodeFinder, Olive AI for healthcare documentation.
   * **Use Cases:** AI generates draft notes, detects missing information, and auto-populates structured data.

2. **Patient Scheduling & Workflow Optimization:**

   * **What AI Does:** Predicts patient no-shows, automates reminders, balances schedules, and reduces patient wait times.
   * **Example Tools:** Qventus, LeanTaaS iQueue, HealthGrid AI Scheduling.
   * **Use Cases:** AI predicts patient cancellations, adjusts provider schedules, and reduces clinic bottlenecks.

3. **Diagnostic & Imaging Assistance:**

   * **What AI Does:** Detects abnormalities in medical images, prioritizes urgent cases, and assists in early disease detection.
   * **Example Tools:** Aidoc, Zebra Medical Vision, Viz.ai, PathAI.
   * **Use Cases:** AI flags critical findings in radiology images and assists in triaging urgent cases for faster intervention.

4. **Remote Patient Monitoring & Telehealth:**

   * **What AI Does:** Analyzes wearable data, detects deterioration signals, and alerts care teams for timely interventions.
   * **Example Tools:** Current Health, Biofourmis, Tempus Insights.
   * **Use Cases:** AI identifies irregular heart rhythms or predicts glucose spikes in diabetic patients.

---

#### **3. Hands-On Exercises & Case Studies (45 minutes)**

**Objective:** Provide learners with practical, hands-on experience using AI tools in real healthcare scenarios.

1. **Case Study 1 — Clinical Documentation & Coding Review:**

   * **Scenario:** You are working at a medical center and need to detect inconsistencies in outpatient visit coding and documentation.
   * **Sample Dataset Columns:**

     ```
     Patient_ID | Visit_Date | Provider | Diagnosis_Notes | Procedure_Notes | Suggested_ICD10 | Suggested_CPT | Billing_Status
     ```
   * **Tasks:**

     * Identify missing or inconsistent ICD-10/CPT codes.
     * Summarize key trends in visit coding errors.
     * Recommend corrections or training focus areas.
   * **Learning Outcomes:** Detect gaps in clinical documentation and coding accuracy.

2. **Case Study 2 — Population Health & Risk Stratification:**

   * **Scenario:** Manage a chronic disease program and prioritize interventions for high-risk patients.
   * **Sample Dataset Columns:**

     ```
     Patient_ID | Age | Gender | Chronic_Conditions | Last_Hospitalization | Lab_Results | Medication_Adherence_Score | Predicted_Risk_Score
     ```
   * **Tasks:**

     * Predict which patients are at highest risk for hospitalization.
     * Suggest intervention priorities and care plans.
     * Group patients by risk tiers for outreach.
   * **Learning Outcomes:** Apply predictive analytics for patient outcomes and optimize care interventions.

3. **Case Study 3 — Scheduling & Operational Simulation:**

   * **Scenario:** Simulate clinic operations to identify bottlenecks and optimize scheduling.
   * **Sample Dataset Columns:**

     ```
     Appointment_ID | Patient_ID | Provider | Appointment_Type | Scheduled_Time | No_Show_Probability | Duration_Minutes | Room_Assigned
     ```
   * **Tasks:**

     * Simulate clinic appointments using AI-based scheduling suggestions.
     * Identify high-risk no-show patients and optimize schedules.
     * Test the impact of extending appointment times or adjusting staffing levels.
   * **Learning Outcomes:** Use AI to improve patient flow and reduce wait times.

---

#### **4. Group Activities / Role-Playing Scenarios (45 minutes)**

**Objective:** Allow learners to apply AI-driven insights in realistic healthcare scenarios through small-group assignments.

1. **Assignment 1 — Clinical Documentation Workflow Redesign:**

   * **Group Task:**

     1. Map the current clinical documentation and coding workflow.
     2. Identify bottlenecks, missing data, or error-prone steps.
     3. Propose an AI-enhanced workflow using documentation/coding AI tools.
   * **Role-Play Variation:** Physician, medical records specialist, coding auditor, billing manager, AI assistant (simulator).

2. **Assignment 2 — Population Health Prioritization Meeting:**

   * **Group Task:**

     1. Analyze the population health dataset.
     2. Identify high-risk patients for intervention.
     3. Design an outreach plan and care pathway prioritization.
   * **Role-Play Variation:** Population health manager, care coordinator, clinician, patient advocate.

3. **Assignment 3 — Scheduling & Operational Simulation:**

   * **Group Task:**

     1. Use AI-based tools to simulate one week of clinic operations.
     2. Optimize the schedule to reduce bottlenecks and no-show rates.
   * **Role-Play Variation:** Operations manager, nurse, provider, patient, AI scheduling assistant.

---

#### **5. Wrap-Up & Reflection (15 minutes)**

**Objective:** Summarize the key learnings and provide a reference sheet for further exploration.

1. **Review Key Concepts:**

   * AI improves efficiency, accuracy, and patient safety in healthcare workflows.
   * AI automates high-volume, repetitive tasks (documentation, coding, scheduling) and supports decision-making (diagnostics, treatment recommendations).
   * Healthcare professionals still provide essential clinical judgment, ethical decisions, and patient communication.

2. **Q&A Session:**

   * Open the floor for final questions or clarifications on AI applications in healthcare.

3. **Closing Remarks:**

   * Encourage learners to explore AI tools in their healthcare settings.
   * Provide additional resources for learners interested in further training on AI in healthcare.
