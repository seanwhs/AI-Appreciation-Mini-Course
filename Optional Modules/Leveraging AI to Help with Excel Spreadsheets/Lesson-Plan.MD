### **Lesson Plan: Leveraging AI to Help with Excel Spreadsheets**

#### **Audience:**

Business professionals, analysts, and anyone looking to enhance their Excel skills using AI to streamline tasks, improve data analysis, and enhance productivity.

#### **Duration:**

**60–90 minutes** (Adjustable based on participant engagement)

---

### **Learning Objectives:**

By the end of this lesson, participants will:

1. Understand how AI can enhance productivity and decision-making in Excel.
2. Learn to leverage AI features in Excel, such as Ideas, Data Types, Forecasting, and AI-driven add-ins.
3. Gain hands-on experience using AI tools for data analysis, error-checking, automation, and predictive analysis.
4. Develop strategies to integrate AI into daily Excel workflows to save time and improve data insights.

---

### **Materials Needed:**

* **Presentation slides** (for visual explanations and demos)
* **Excel (Microsoft 365)** on computers or laptops with AI features enabled
* **Handouts** (summary of AI tools and add-ins for Excel)
* **Whiteboard or flip chart** (for group discussions)
* **Sample data sets** (for hands-on exercises)

---

### **Lesson Outline:**

---

### **1. Introduction to AI and Excel (10-15 minutes)**

**A. What is AI? (5 minutes)**

* **Definition of AI**: Briefly introduce Artificial Intelligence and its ability to perform tasks requiring human-like intelligence.
* **AI in Excel**: Highlight how AI can automate repetitive tasks, assist with data analysis, and help users make data-driven decisions quickly.

**Discussion Prompt:**

* Have you used AI tools before? How do you think AI can enhance your Excel experience?

**B. Overview of AI Features in Excel (5–10 minutes)**

* Introduce AI features like **Ideas in Excel**, **Forecast Sheet**, **Data Types**, and **AI-powered Excel add-ins**.
* Briefly explain their benefits in data management, forecasting, and decision-making.

**Visual Demo:**

* Show a quick demo of the **Ideas** feature in Excel and how it can provide insights into a simple dataset.

---

### **2. AI-Powered Features in Excel (20–25 minutes)**

**A. Ideas in Excel (10 minutes)**

* **Explanation**: Demonstrate how the **Ideas** feature analyzes data and provides insights, patterns, and recommended charts or pivot tables.
* **Activity**: Have participants open a sample data set and use **Ideas** to generate insights.
* **Discussion**: After the activity, discuss how the feature suggested insights and how participants can use this in their own work.

**Activity:**

1. Select a dataset in Excel.
2. Click the **Ideas** button under the **Home** tab.
3. Explore the insights and visualizations suggested by Excel.

**B. Data Types and Dynamic Data (10 minutes)**

* **Explanation**: Show how Excel can recognize and convert text into dynamic data types (e.g., geography, stocks).
* **Activity**: Participants will input a list of country names or stock symbols and convert them into dynamic data.
* **Discussion**: Discuss how this feature can make data management easier by automatically updating information from online sources.

**Activity:**

1. Enter a list of country names or stock symbols in a column.
2. Go to **Insert > Data Types** (Geography/Stocks) and convert the column.
3. View the live data that Excel links to these entries.

---

### **3. Predictive Analysis with AI (15 minutes)**

**A. Forecasting with AI (5 minutes)**

* **Explanation**: Introduce the **Forecast Sheet** feature that uses machine learning to predict future trends based on historical data.
* **Activity**: Demonstrate how to create a forecast for future values using the **Forecast Sheet** option in Excel.

**Activity:**

1. Select a data range (e.g., past sales data).
2. Go to **Data > Forecast Sheet**.
3. Review the prediction and insights provided by Excel.

**Discussion:**

* How accurate did you find the AI predictions for your data? How might this feature be useful in your work?

---

### **4. Automating Tasks with AI in Excel (10–15 minutes)**

**A. Using Macros and Power Automate (5–10 minutes)**

* **Explanation**: Demonstrate how to automate repetitive tasks using **Macros** and **Power Automate** integration in Excel.
* **Activity**: Show how to record a basic macro to automate a simple task (e.g., formatting a table).
* **Activity**: Demonstrate a simple automation workflow using **Power Automate** (e.g., sending a report email based on specific conditions in Excel).

**Activity 1: Record a Macro**

1. Select data that needs formatting.
2. Record a macro to automate the formatting process.
3. Save and apply the macro.

**Activity 2: Automate with Power Automate**

1. Set up a simple automation task (e.g., send an email when a value exceeds a certain threshold).
2. Test the workflow and review the results.

**Discussion:**

* How might automation save you time in your Excel tasks? What tasks do you wish could be automated?

---

### **5. Data Cleansing and Error Checking with AI (10 minutes)**

**A. AI-Driven Error Detection (5 minutes)**

* **Explanation**: Demonstrate how Excel uses AI to detect common data issues, such as mismatched data types or missing values.
* **Activity**: Show participants how to use Excel’s **Error Checking** tools and **Data Validation** features to identify potential issues in their data.

**Activity:**

1. Open a sample dataset with common errors (e.g., blank cells, inconsistent data).
2. Use Excel’s built-in tools to detect and correct errors.

**Discussion:**

* How effective do you find AI in detecting errors and inconsistencies in your data?

---

### **6. Best Practices for Integrating AI into Your Excel Workflow (5 minutes)**

**A. Tips for Efficient Use of AI in Excel (5 minutes)**

* **Keep Data Clean**: AI works best with well-organized data. Regularly clean your data before applying AI features.
* **Start Small**: Begin with simpler AI tools like **Ideas** and **Forecast Sheet** before exploring more complex integrations.
* **Combine AI with Existing Skills**: Combine AI features with traditional Excel functions (e.g., pivot tables, formulas) for more powerful results.

**Quick Tips:**

* Use AI to automate repetitive tasks.
* Regularly check for data issues and clean your data for better AI performance.

---

### **7. Conclusion & Next Steps (5–10 minutes)**

**A. Recap and Key Takeaways**

* AI tools in Excel can automate tedious tasks, provide predictive insights, and help analyze large datasets more effectively.
* AI-driven features like **Ideas**, **Forecast Sheet**, and **Data Types** can significantly improve your data management and analysis capabilities.

**B. Next Steps for Participants**

* Explore the AI features covered in this lesson on your own Excel workbooks.
* Start using AI tools like **Ideas** and **Forecast Sheet** in your daily workflow.
* Experiment with **Power Automate** to automate tasks and streamline your processes.

**Q&A Session**:
Open the floor for any questions or further clarifications.

---

### **Assessment:**

* **Formative Assessment**: Participation in hands-on activities, use of AI features in Excel, and discussions.
* **Optional Follow-up**: Ask participants to apply AI tools to a real-world project or dataset and share the results.
