# **Lesson Plan: AI and Its Subfields**

## **Module Duration:** 4–5 hours (can be split into multiple sessions)

## **Target Audience:** Beginners to intermediate learners interested in AI

## **Learning Objectives:**

By the end of this lesson, participants will be able to:

1. Define Artificial Intelligence (AI) and explain its subfields: ML, DL, Generative AI, and Agentic AI.
2. Understand the differences between these AI subfields.
3. Identify practical applications for each subfield.
4. Experiment with basic AI tools and models.
5. Discuss ethical considerations and societal implications of AI.

---

## **Session Outline**

### **Session 1: Introduction to AI (30 minutes)**

**Topics:**

* What is AI?
* Narrow AI vs General AI vs Superintelligent AI

**Activities:**

* Icebreaker discussion: “Where do you see AI in your daily life?”
* Short demo: Voice assistant or recommendation system

**Materials:**

* Slides with diagrams
* Video examples of AI in action

---

### **Session 2: Machine Learning (ML) (45 minutes)**

**Topics:**

* Definition and key concept: learning patterns from data
* Types of ML: Supervised, Unsupervised, Reinforcement Learning
* Practical applications: predictions, recommendation systems, fraud detection

**Activities:**

* Case study discussion: How Netflix recommends movies
* Quick interactive quiz: Identify the type of ML in given examples

**Materials:**

* Slides with examples
* Small datasets for demonstration

---

### **Session 3: Deep Learning (DL) (45 minutes)**

**Topics:**

* Definition: Neural networks with multiple layers for complex tasks
* Key concepts: neurons, layers, weights, activation functions, backpropagation
* Applications: computer vision, NLP, speech recognition, autonomous vehicles

**Activities:**

* Show a CNN classifying images or a transformer model for text generation
* Group discussion: Identify tasks in participants’ fields suitable for DL

**Materials:**

* Diagram slides of neural networks
* Pre-trained model demos

---

### **Session 4: Generative AI (GenAI) (30 minutes)**

**Topics:**

* Definition: AI that generates new content
* Key models: GANs, Transformers
* Applications: text, image, music, video

**Activities:**

* Hands-on demo: Generate text with ChatGPT or an image with DALL-E
* Group reflection: Discuss creative applications in business or art

**Materials:**

* Access to AI tools
* Example outputs for discussion

---

### **Session 5: Agentic AI (30 minutes)**

**Topics:**

* Definition: Autonomous, goal-directed AI
* Key characteristics: decision-making, planning, adaptation
* Applications: autonomous vehicles, robotic systems, agent simulations

**Activities:**

* Discussion: Risks and benefits of agentic AI
* Simulation demo: AutoGPT or BabyAGI performing tasks autonomously

**Materials:**

* Slides with examples
* Access to agentic AI simulation tools

---

### **Session 6: Ethical Considerations (20–30 minutes)**

**Topics:**

* Bias and fairness in AI models
* Privacy and data security
* Explainability of AI decisions
* Risks of misuse and societal impact

**Activities:**

* Case discussion: AI loan approvals and biased outcomes
* Group brainstorming: Strategies to ensure responsible AI use

**Materials:**

* Handouts or slides with ethical scenarios

---

### **Session 7: Hands-On Mini Projects (60–90 minutes)**

**Activities:**

1. **ML Experiment:** Train a simple supervised model (e.g., predict student grades or classify flowers).
2. **DL Experiment:** Use a pre-trained CNN for image classification.
3. **GenAI Experiment:** Generate text, image, or music using AI tools.
4. **Agentic AI Simulation:** Observe an autonomous agent completing tasks.

**Materials:**

* Laptops with internet access
* Google Colab notebooks or other platforms
* Pre-loaded datasets and models

---

### **Session 8: Wrap-Up and Reflection (15–20 minutes)**

**Activities:**

* Recap key concepts and subfields
* Showcase mini project results
* Q&A and discussion: “How will you use AI responsibly in your field?”

---

### **Assessment / Evaluation**

* Participation in discussions and activities
* Completion of at least one mini AI experiment
* Reflection on ethical implications

---

### **Resources Needed**

* Computers with internet access
* Google Colab or Jupyter Notebooks
* Pre-trained AI models (TensorFlow, PyTorch, Hugging Face Transformers)
* Generative AI tools (ChatGPT, DALL-E, MidJourney)
* Agentic AI simulators (AutoGPT, BabyAGI)
